Protect Your Security Deposit

Your deposit isn’t a fee. It’s your money — temporarily held.

What Is a Security Deposit?

A security deposit is money you give your landlord before moving in. It protects them in case of unpaid rent or property damage.

If you follow your lease and leave the place in good condition, that money should come back to you.

Why It Matters

For many renters, the deposit equals one full month of rent. That’s not a small amount.

Protecting it means protecting your savings and your ability to move comfortably when the time comes.

How Deposits Are Lost

  • Unpaid rent or late fees
  • Damage beyond normal wear and tear
  • Breaking the lease early
  • Not giving proper move-out notice

Most deposit losses aren’t dramatic. They’re small things that add up.

How To Protect It

  • Take photos of the apartment before moving in
  • Report existing damage in writing
  • Pay rent on time
  • Keep communication documented
  • Give written notice before moving out
  • Clean thoroughly before leaving

Think of it as keeping records, not defending yourself. Documentation creates clarity.

Normal Wear vs. Damage

Normal wear and tear includes small scuffs, minor carpet wear, or faded paint over time.

Damage includes holes in walls, broken fixtures, or anything caused by neglect.

Knowing the difference prevents unnecessary disputes.

Progress Check

  • ☐ I understand how much my deposit is
  • ☐ I know what can legally be deducted
  • ☐ I plan to document move-in condition
  • ☐ I understand move-out notice requirements
