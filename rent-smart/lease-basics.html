Lease Basics

Understanding your lease protects your money, your stability, and your future housing options.

What Is a Lease?

A lease is a written agreement between you and your landlord. It explains how much rent you pay, how long you’ll stay, and what responsibilities both sides have.

It might feel like paperwork, but it actually sets the foundation for your housing stability.

Why It Matters

Your lease controls important parts of your financial life:

  • Your monthly rent payment
  • Late fees
  • Your security deposit
  • How and when you can move out

When you understand your lease, you avoid surprises and protect your finances.

Common Mistakes

  • Signing without reading carefully
  • Assuming every lease is the same
  • Not understanding early termination rules

Many renters make these mistakes — learning this now already puts you ahead.

Key Takeaways

  • A lease is a legally binding contract
  • It controls rent, deposits, and move-out rules
  • Reading it carefully protects your money

Progress Check

  • ☐ I understand my rent amount and due date
  • ☐ I reviewed late fee rules
  • ☐ I know my security deposit terms
  • ☐ I understand how to end my lease
