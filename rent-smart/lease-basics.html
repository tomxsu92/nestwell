What Is a Lease?

A lease is a written agreement between you and your landlord. It explains what you agree to pay, how long you’ll stay, and what both sides are responsible for.

It’s not just paperwork. It’s the foundation of your housing stability. Before signing anything, it’s worth slowing down and understanding what it says.

Why It Matters

Your lease affects your monthly budget, your security deposit, your credit (if payments are missed), and your ability to move out early.

When you understand your lease, you protect your money and your future options. Clarity removes stress.

What To Look For

  • Rent amount and due date
  • Late fees
  • Security deposit terms
  • Maintenance responsibilities
  • Early termination rules
  • Renewal terms

If something feels unclear, ask questions. That’s not being difficult — that’s being responsible.

Common Mistakes

  • Skimming the lease quickly
  • Assuming everything is standard
  • Forgetting to check move-out rules

You’re not behind if you’ve done this before. You’re learning now — and that’s what matters.

Take 10 Minutes

Set aside 10 quiet minutes. Read your current lease (or a sample one). Highlight anything you don’t fully understand.

Small steps build confidence.

Progress Check

  • ☐ I understand my rent amount and due date
  • ☐ I reviewed late fee rules
  • ☐ I know my security deposit terms
  • ☐ I understand how to end my lease
