How Often Should Renters Replace Air Filters? (And What Happens If You Don’t)

If you're renting and wondering whether you're supposed to replace the air filter — you're not alone. A lot of renters ignore it because they assume it's the landlord’s job. Sometimes it is. Sometimes it isn’t.

Here's the simple rule: Most renters should replace their air filter every 1–3 months.

Why It Matters (Even If You Don’t Own the Place)

Your air filter controls airflow through your HVAC system. When it gets clogged, air can’t move properly. That creates problems fast.

  • Your apartment gets hotter or colder than normal
  • Your energy bill increases
  • The system works harder and can break down
  • Dust builds up everywhere

And if the HVAC system gets damaged because of a clogged filter? That can become your responsibility — especially if your lease says basic upkeep is yours.

How Often Should You Actually Replace It?

Here’s the practical breakdown:

  • Every 1 month – If you have pets or allergies
  • Every 2 months – Normal apartment use
  • Every 3 months – Light usage, no pets

If you’re not sure — check it once a month. Hold it up to the light. If you can’t see through it clearly, replace it.

Is It Your Responsibility or the Landlord’s?

This depends on your lease. Many leases say renters are responsible for “routine maintenance,” which usually includes air filters.

If you're unsure:

  • Check your lease under maintenance responsibilities
  • Email maintenance and ask directly
  • Ask your property manager

Even if it’s technically their job, replacing a $10 filter is often easier than dealing with a broken AC in July.

How to Replace an Apartment Air Filter (It’s Easier Than You Think)

  1. Turn off your HVAC system
  2. Locate the return vent (usually on wall or ceiling)
  3. Remove the old filter
  4. Check the size printed on the frame
  5. Insert the new filter facing the airflow arrow correctly
  6. Turn system back on

That’s it. Five minutes. No tools required in most apartments.

What Happens If You Don’t Replace It?

Here’s what actually happens:

  • Reduced airflow
  • Frozen AC coils
  • Higher electricity bills
  • Possible HVAC damage

Worst case? Maintenance determines neglect caused the issue. That’s not a fun conversation during move-out inspection.

Bottom Line

Replacing your air filter every 1–3 months is one of the cheapest ways to protect your comfort and your deposit.

Don’t overthink it. Check it monthly. Replace when dirty. It’s simple preventative maintenance.
